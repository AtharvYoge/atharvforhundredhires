## Post 2
Date: [18 April 2026]
Engagement: [33 likes]

The people on my team who adapted to AI fastest all had one thing in common:
They already knew how to delegate.

And the ones struggling? They'd spent their careers being the person who executed — not the one who directed.

Here's what I've realized: AI is just delegation. Same rules apply.

The framework is attributed to Steve Jobs, John Maxwell, Dan Martell and more...

It's called 10-80-10:
10% — You do the research, context, and direction upfront.
80% — AI executes the work.
10% — You review, tweak, and finalize.

Bad delegator: "Hey, write me an email sequence."
Good delegator: "Here are 40 swipe files. Here are the 3 best books on this topic. Here's every training I've ever done on it. Build me a process that produces perfect output every time."

For every 1 word you want back from AI, you need to put in 500 words of context.

Most people are trying to get a 1,000-word sales letter by typing 10 words. Then they say AI doesn't work.

The bottleneck was never the AI. It was the delegation skill.

The good news: it's learnable. One of our guys coded his own software that took him from contacting a dozen affiliates a day to 300 — all in the same amount of time. That's what happens when you master the 10%.

If you've been frustrated with your AI results — before blaming the tool, ask yourself: did I put in the 10%?

## Post 3

I almost quit ClickFunnels in year one.
We had burned through our savings. My wife was worried. I was working 16-hour days and the numbers weren't moving.
I remember sitting in my office at 2am staring at a spreadsheet thinking… maybe this was a mistake.
What kept me going wasn't motivation.
It wasn't a vision board or a podcast or a morning routine.
It was one sentence I had read in a 100-year-old sales book:
"The man who masters the art of service will never want for customers."
That was it. That was the shift.
I had been so focused on building the product that I forgot to obsess over the customer.
Once I flipped that switch — everything changed.
ClickFunnels crossed $100M in revenue without a single investor.
Not because we were the smartest people in the room.
Because we stopped asking "how do we grow?" and started asking "how do we serve?"
That question will take you further than any growth hack ever will.

If you're in the middle of a hard season right now — stay in it.
The answer is usually simpler than you think.
Serve better. Sell more. Repeat.

## Post 4
Date : [15 April 2026]
Engagement: [112 Likes]

In 2014 my co-founder Todd and I launched ClickFunnels with no investors, no VC backing, and no guarantee it would work.
Most people don't know what happened the first week.
We had 9 signups.
Not 9,000. Not 900.
Nine.
I remember sitting at my desk thinking — did we just make the biggest mistake of our lives?
Here's what I didn't know then:
Nine people who desperately needed what you built is more valuable than nine million people who sort of like what you made.
We talked to those nine people every single day.
We built exactly what they told us they needed.
We didn't chase the market. We served the room.
Ten years later those nine signups turned into 100,000+ active users and over $1B in revenue — bootstrapped.
The lesson isn't "think big."
The lesson is "serve small first."
The scale takes care of itself when the foundation is right.
