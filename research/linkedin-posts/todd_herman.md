## Post 1
Date: [3 Months Ago]
Link: [https://www.linkedin.com/posts/toddherman1_we-live-in-one-of-the-most-weirdly-absurd-activity-7417290938416406528-8R1c?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADV5al8BQgB4-ooi4L8oI09iRGyq9rxBA1A]

We live in one of the most weirdly absurd times. 

We have more answers than any generation in history, and yet, I’ve never seen leaders feel more lost.

I get the opportunity to straddle the world of elite pro sports, and top-level business leaders, which gives me a unique opportunity. 

And, there’s this new phenomenon happening.

Yes, a language model can tell you what to do. But, it can’t see who you are in this moment. It can’t notice that you’ve drifted away from yourself. 

It has no way of saying, “Wait—you seem different today. What’s actually going on?”

This is what a human can do. Not dispense information, but witness. To hold the whole of you while you fumble through the particulars.

The issue is becoming so evident, because it hasn’t shown up with the athletes I coach the past 18 months…yet.

Their inner world is still largely untouched by AI. LLMs can’t go and score the goal. The athletes value still exists under the white hot light of observable performance.

But business leaders can manufacture performance, call it their own, and wonder why success feels hollow.

The hijacking of people’s coping systems has begun, and they’re losing touch with their own wisdom.

## Post 5
Date: [1 year Ago]
Link:[https://www.linkedin.com/posts/toddherman1_turns-out-the-words-people-use-to-brand-activity-7296651927088418816-VhUP?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADV5al8BQgB4-ooi4L8oI09iRGyq9rxBA1A]

Turns out, the words people use to brand themselves online can predict whether or not they’ll skip out on a payment plan. Here’s how I found out...

For 4 years, my 90 Day Year training company executed some of the biggest campaigns. And a significant channel for us was affiliates.

In just one campaign, my coaching client, Amy Porterfield, did $780k+ herself. (400+ affiliates were promoting for that campaign.)

As a part of the offer, we gave people a payment plan option. Pretty common right??? 

The problem is, that some people took advantage of it and never completed their payments. Or tried to execute chargebacks after the refund period.

Now, because I like data and I view everything as a learning opportunity, I asked the team:

1) Go to the social profiles of these people,
2) Grab the text from their bios,
3) Paste it into a Google Sheet.

The reason:

I wanted our marketing team to get the information so we could repel that kind of buyer in the future.

Then, we placed all the information into 'tagcrowd[dot]com' and created a word cloud with the most common words.

What do you think they were???

#1 - Heart-Centered
#2 - Spiritual

It didn't surprise me.

If you'd like a fun little exercise this week... do it for your business and see what you come up with.

I shared this with a friend, and they used the same process for the people they disliked the most on social media. The #1 word she found in their bios -'Serial Entrepreneur.' 

Have fun with it... and hopefully, it makes your world a bit better.
