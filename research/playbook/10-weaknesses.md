## Weaknesses of This Playbook

---

## 1. Assumes you have an audience or can build one quickly

The LinkedIn organic strategy in Phase 3 takes weeks to gain momentum.
If you need to run your first webinar next week with zero followers,
you will struggle without paid ads or a cold outreach budget. This
playbook does not cover paid traffic at all that is a significant gap.

## 2. Assumes your email tool is already set up

This playbook does not cover technical setup which email platform to
use, how to connect it to your registration page, or how to automatically
segment registrants from no shows. A production ready SOP would need
a full technical setup section.

## 3. Conversion benchmarks partly come from consumer market experts

Much of the conversion rate data from Russell Brunson and Amy Porterfield
is based on audiences of entrepreneurs and online course buyers, not
B2B SaaS buyers. B2B buyers behave differently. The benchmarks here are
starting points, not guarantees. Nathan Barry's ConvertKit data is the
most directly relevant, but his audience was bloggers and creators,
not enterprise SaaS.

## 4. This playbook has not been personally tested

Everything here is synthesised from research, not from running an actual
webinar funnel. The ideas are well sourced and logically sound, but the
only real test is execution. Results will vary.

## 5. Does not cover what happens after someone books a call

For higher ticket SaaS, the webinar leads to a sales call, not a direct
purchase. This playbook ends at conversion intent. The sales call,
the proposal, and the close are an entirely separate SOP that is
not covered here.
