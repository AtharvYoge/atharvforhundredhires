## Step 3.1 — LinkedIn organic is your best free channel for B2B SaaS

Post about your webinar topic for two weeks before the event.
Do not promote the webinar directly every day that looks like spam.

Ratio: 4 posts about the problem your webinar solves, 1 post promoting
the webinar itself.

Example post structure:
- Post 1: "The biggest mistake I see SaaS founders make with onboarding..."
- Post 2: "A framework for reducing churn in the first 30 days..."
- Post 3: "Why most onboarding emails get ignored..."
- Post 4: "What actually works instead..."
- Post 5: "I am running a free 60 minute training on this next Tuesday.
  Link in comments."

Source: Rand Fishkin, LinkedIn content strategy,
https://sparktoro.com/blog/how-do-we-anchor-content-marketing-efforts-in-a-multi-platform-distribution-world/— September 2024

## Step 3.2 — Affiliate driven webinars are the fastest way to fill a room from zero

Nathan Barry grew ConvertKit from $1,500 MRR to $98,000 MRR in one year
by running 150+ webinars with affiliate partners people who already had
the audience he wanted. One webinar with Pat Flynn generated 1,000 signups
in 24 hours. The webinars were educational, not sales pitches. Attendees
got free courses, ebooks, and a free trial. Trust was built before any
purchase was asked for.

The lesson for B2B SaaS: find 5 people who already serve your ideal
customer and offer to run a free educational webinar for their audience.
You provide the content. They provide the room.

Source: Nathan Barry, "Growing ConvertKit to $30,000 in Monthly Recurring Revenue,"
https://nathanbarry.com/30k/ — published October 2015

## Step 3.3 — Cold email can fill your first webinar if you have no audience

Send a personalised cold email to 200 ideal customers one week before.
Subject line: "Free training for [job title] on [specific topic] — [date], 60 min"

Keep the email under 100 words. One link. No attachments.

Source: Alex Berman, cold outreach and webinar combo strategy,
https://alexberman.com/email-outreach-strategy — March 2026
