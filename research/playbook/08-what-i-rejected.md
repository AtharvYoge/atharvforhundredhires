## What I Rejected and Why

---

## Rejected idea 1: Offer stacking

Offer stacking is a technique from Russell Brunson and Jason Fladlien
where you add multiple bonuses to your pitch, each with an inflated
"retail value," to make the total package seem like an obvious deal.

Example: "You get the course ($997 value), the templates ($497 value),
the coaching call ($297 value) — all for just $997 today."

Why I rejected it: This works in consumer markets where buyers make
emotional, fast decisions. In B2B SaaS, buyers spend company money,
need manager approval and compare you against competitors. Inflated
retail values on bonuses damage credibility with professional buyers
who see through the numbers immediately. It signals consumer style
marketing applied to a B2B context, which creates distrust, not urgency.

For B2B SaaS the pitch should be built on ROI, real case studies,
and a clear next step not bonus stacking.

---

## Rejected idea 2: Evergreen webinars presented as live

Several sources in the ClickFunnels ecosystem recommend recording one
webinar and running it on a loop, presenting it as happening live with
fake participant counts and scripted chat responses.

Why I rejected it: B2B buyers research vendors thoroughly. They talk to
each other in Slack communities and at events. If a buyer attends your
"live" webinar and later discovers it was a recording, they will not
trust your product. The deception is increasingly well-known.

A recorded webinar presented honestly as on-demand training is fine
and can convert well. Presenting a recording as a live event is not.

Nathan Barry ran real live webinars at ConvertKit sometimes 2 to 3
per day. That is why they built real trust, not just registrations.
