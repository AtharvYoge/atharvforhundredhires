## Phase 5: Run the Webinar

---

## Step 5.1 — The first 10 minutes decide everything

Most attendees decide in the first 10 minutes whether to stay or leave.
Use this structure for your opening:

1. Re state the promise: "In the next 60 minutes you will learn X"
2. Establish credibility in 60 seconds: one result, one number, one story
3. Preview the three things you will cover
4. Tell them to stay until the end: "I am sharing something at the end
   that I do not put anywhere else"

Source: Russell Brunson, Perfect Webinar opening structure,
https://www.clickfunnels.com/blog/complete-guide-high-converting-webinar/ — March 2026

---

## Step 5.2 — Structure the content in three "secrets"

Do not teach everything you know. Teach three specific insights that
each break a false belief your audience holds about the problem.

False belief 1 → Your reframe → Mini case study
False belief 2 → Your reframe → Mini case study
False belief 3 → Your reframe → Mini case study

After the third insight, transition to your offer.

Source: Jason Fladlien, webinar scripting methodology,
https://www.linkedin.com/in/jasonfladlien/ — April 2026

---

## Step 5.3 — The transition to your offer should not feel like a pivot

Do not say "okay now I want to tell you about my product."

Say: "Everything I just showed you is what I do manually.
The reason I built [product] is so you never have to do it manually again."

The webinar content becomes the reason the product exists.

Source: Wes Bush, product-led webinar structure,
https://www.linkedin.com/in/wesbush/ — April 2026

---

## Step 5.4 — Keep the pitch to 15 minutes maximum

In B2B SaaS, a long pitch kills credibility.
Your buyers are professionals. Respect their time.

What to cover in your 15-minute pitch:
1. What the product does (one sentence)
2. Who it is for (be specific)
3. What results customers have seen (two to three examples with numbers)
4. What they get if they sign up today
5. The price and what happens next

Source: Todd Herman, high-ticket webinar close structure,
https://www.linkedin.com/in/toddlherman/ — collected April 2025

---

## Step 5.5 — Educational webinars convert better than sales webinars in B2B

Nathan Barry's insight from ConvertKit's 150+ webinar run: the webinars
that converted best gave away the most value upfront. They included free
courses, tools, and trials. The product was mentioned at the end, almost
as a footnote. Attendees converted because they already trusted the brand
by the time the offer was made.

For B2B SaaS: the more your webinar teaches, the more your product sells.

Source: Nathan Barry, direct sales and webinar strategy for ConvertKit,
https://nathanbarry.com/sales/ — published February 2019, collected April 2025
