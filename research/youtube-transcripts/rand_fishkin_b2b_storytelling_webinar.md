## Video title: B2B Storytelling by Rand Fishkin and Jay Cunzo

---
## Key insights from this video
- Rather than staying on the 'marketing hamster wheel' by churning out high-volume, generic content, focus on creating work that is both insightful and personal. Impact is driven by how well you empower your audience with actionable perspectives rather than just providing basic information.
- To connect more deeply, use a simple three-part structure to frame your content:
(1) This happened (a personal memory or observation)
(2) which made me realize (the pivot to an idea)
(3) that's the thing about... (the core insight or lesson you are teaching). This helps transition from a personal anecdote to professional value.
- B2B marketing often focuses too heavily on getting in front of as many people as possible. Instead, aim to have a smaller, more dedicated group of people declare your brand as their favorite. By turning inward to find your unique premise defensible assertion based on your perspective you build a brand that people actually care about.
